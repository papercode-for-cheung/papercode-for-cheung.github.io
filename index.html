Short Bio

Peiliang Zhang is pursuing his Ph.D. degree in the School of Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence at Wuhan University of Technology (武汉理工大学), Wuhan, China, supervised by Prof. Jingling Yuan. He is also currently a visiting Ph.D. student in Yonsei DataLab supervised by Associate Prof. Yongjun Zhu at Yonsei University (延世大学), Seoul, South Korea. Before that, in 2022, he received his M.S. degree in software engineering from Dalian University (大连大学), Dalian, China, supervised by Prof. Chao Che in Key Laboratory of Advanced Design and Intelligent Computing, Ministry of Education.

His research interest broadly lies in the areas of AI for Science, Molecular Representation and Medical Informatics. He has published more than 10 papers in high quality conferences and journals, such as DASFAA, IEEE BIBM, ESWA, etc. He has also served as a reviewer of multiple conferences and journals, including AAAI, TII, and bioinformatics.

DASFAA 2024
Key Substructure Learning with Chemical Intuition for Material Property Prediction
Peiliang Zhang, Jingling Yuan, Lin Li, Wen Luo, Jiwei Hu, Xin Li
PDF PPT
The paper proposed the key substructure learning with chemical intuition. KSCI is used to capture irregular substructures within molecules and to identify the differential contributions of these substructures to chemical properties.
ESWA 2024
NCH-DDA: Neighborhood Contrastive Learning Heterogeneous Network for Drug-Disease Association Prediction
Peiliang Zhang, Chao Che, Bo Jin, Jingling Yuan, Ruixin Li, Yongjun Zhu
Code PDF
The paper proposed the Neighborhood Contrastive Learning for Heterogeneous Networks, which integrates information from multiple domains such as drugs and diseases. NCH-DDA enhances the generalization ability of drug repositioning models and shows greater potential for application in sparse data scenarios.
